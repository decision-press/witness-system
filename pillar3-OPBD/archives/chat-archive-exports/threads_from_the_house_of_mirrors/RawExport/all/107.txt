# **Code Blue Lock Down – Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)**

## **Mission Statement**
Provide **three minutes of protection** until police arrive by:  
1. Rapidly securing the building.  
2. Shielding children and congregation.  
3. Maintaining calm and control.  

---

## **1. Pre-Service Preparation (All Volunteers)**
- Arrive **30 minutes before service**.  
- Check that all **non-foyer doors are locked** (emergency exit only).  
- Test **radios** and confirm security channel is clear.  
- Security Lead assigns posts:  
  - **Foyer Team (2)** – monitor main entrances.  
  - **Sanctuary Team (2)** – near sanctuary interior doors.  
  - **Children’s Team (2+)** – posted near classroom hall.  
  - **Hall/Admin Team (2)** – cover gathering hall and offices.  
  - **Floater(s)** – backup responders.  
- Review **code word/phrase** for lockdown.  
- Confirm **911 call procedure** (who calls, who covers if unavailable).  

---

## **2. Lockdown Trigger (First Recognition of Threat)**
- **Step 1 – Foyer Team**:  
  - Identify threat.  
  - Immediately announce **lockdown code phrase** over radio.  
  - One member **calls 911** (provide address, “active shooter, armed suspect inside/outside,” # of people present).  
- **Step 2 – All Teams**:  
  - Secure assigned zone.  
  - Report status back to Security Lead (“Sanctuary secured,” “Classrooms locked”).  

---

## **3. Lockdown Execution by Zone**
### **Children’s Classrooms**
- Teachers lock classroom doors immediately.  
- Lights off, blinds down.  
- Move children away from windows and doors, into corner out of line of sight.  
- Maintain silence until all-clear.  

### **Sanctuary**
- Sanctuary Team secures interior doors to foyer and side exits.  
- Ushers quietly direct congregation to move away from foyer side.  
- Encourage silence, crouch low, remain in place.  

### **Foyer**
- Foyer Team uses available cover, does not attempt pursuit.  
- Delay intruder if possible by locking, barricading, or retreating while securing.  
- Communicate intruder’s location/direction over radio.  

### **Gathering Hall &amp; Admin Offices**
- Lock doors.  
- Move occupants away from windows and glass.  
- Direct to sheltering corners.  

---

## **4. Communication Protocol**
- Use **short, clear radio calls**:  
  - “Code Blue, sanctuary secured.”  
  - “Code Blue, classrooms locked.”  
  - “Shooter in foyer, moving east.”  
- Security Lead maintains calm tone and keeps updates flowing.  
- No chatter — report only facts.  

---

## **5. During Lockdown (3-Minute Defense Window)**
- Stay in cover and locked rooms.  
- Do not open doors for anyone except police.  
- Floaters reinforce weak points if safe.  
- Continue silent 911 communication if possible (line open).  

---

## **6. Police Arrival**
- Security Lead or designated greeter meets police at **safe entry point** (not foyer).  
- Provide map of building and threat location if known.  
- Volunteers stand down when ordered by police.  

---

## **7. Post-Incident**
- Account for all children and adults.  
- Medical response for injured.  
- Debrief with volunteers (what worked, what failed).  
- Update SOP if needed.  

---

# **Quick Checklist for Volunteers**
✅ Know your zone.  
✅ Lockdown within 60 seconds.  
✅ Call 911 immediately.  
✅ Keep congregation calm.  
✅ Protect for three minutes until law enforcement takes over.
