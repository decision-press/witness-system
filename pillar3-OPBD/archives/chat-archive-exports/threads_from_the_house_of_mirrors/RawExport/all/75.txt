## Sidebar: Two Modes of Rhetoric – *Unhumans* vs. Hirschman  

Albert O. Hirschman’s *The Rhetoric of Reaction* identified three standard strategies of reactionary argument: **perversity, futility, and jeopardy**. Each is a way rhetoric resists reform by claiming it backfires, changes nothing, or endangers what already exists.  

The 2024 polemic *Unhumans* by Jack Posobiec and Joshua Lisec demonstrates these strategies in action.  

- **Perversity** → *Unhumans* frames leftist or revolutionary movements as inherently self-defeating: they claim to liberate but inevitably enslave.  
- **Futility** → It suggests moderation or compromise with such movements is pointless: “the playbook” always wins unless utterly destroyed.  
- **Jeopardy** → It insists the very survival of Western civilization is at stake, that all existing goods (freedom, family, faith, nation) are on the verge of collapse.  

Hirschman analyzed rhetoric to expose it. *Unhumans* deploys rhetoric to inflame. Both deal in repetition and pattern — but one reveals, the other weaponizes.  

This juxtaposition clarifies a central danger: **when truth is stripped of context and presented with vitriol, truth itself becomes a lie.** What Hirschman treated as a scholarly pattern of speech, *Unhumans* elevates into a battlefield manual.  

For *The God Decision*, the lesson is plain: belief can be turned into cruelty if trust and discernment are abandoned. Rhetoric without integrity erodes faith; rhetoric anchored in truth and patience can preserve it.
