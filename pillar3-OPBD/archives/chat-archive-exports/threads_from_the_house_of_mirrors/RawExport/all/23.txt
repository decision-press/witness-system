- a **USB-MIDI interface** (sending/receiving MIDI data directly to your computer), and  
- a **24-bit/96 kHz USB audio interface** (sending/receiving audio to your DAW).  

So yes — you can absolutely go **USB-B → your PC’s USB-A (or USB-C with adapter)** without needing extra 5-pin DIN MIDI cables *if* you’re using the computer as the bridge.

Here’s your streamlined setup for **direct USB-to-Fantom MIDI control**:  

---

### ⚙️ Hardware
- **Cable:** USB-B (printer-style) → USB-A or USB-C (depending on your PC port).  
  Plug the **square end** into the Fantom’s **USB COMPUTER** port (not the one labeled MEMORY).  

---

### 🧠 Fantom Settings
1. On the Fantom, press **Menu → System → USB**.  
2. Set **USB Driver = VENDOR**.  
   - (If you ever just want class-compliant plug-and-play with no driver, “Generic” works — but we want full MIDI control, so Vendor mode is better.)  
3. Press **Write** to save, then **reboot** the Fantom.  

---

### 💻 Computer Setup
1. Download and install the **Roland FANTOM USB Driver** for your OS (Windows or macOS):  
   👉 [https://www.roland.com/support/](https://www.roland.com/support/)  
   (Search “Fantom-8” and grab the latest USB driver for your system.)  
2. After install, plug the USB cable back in — your PC should detect a new MIDI interface called **FANTOM-8**.  
3. Verify:
   - **Windows:** Open *Device Manager → Sound, Video, and Game Controllers* → You should see “Roland FANTOM-8.”  
   - **Mac:** In *Audio MIDI Setup → MIDI Studio*, it should appear automatically.  

---

### 🔄 Test Connection
If you’ve got any DAW or even a free MIDI monitor tool (like *MIDI-OX* on Windows or *MIDI Monitor* on Mac):  
- Play a key on the Fantom — you should see MIDI messages incoming.  
- Send a test note or sequence to **FANTOM-8 OUT** — the Fantom should play it.
