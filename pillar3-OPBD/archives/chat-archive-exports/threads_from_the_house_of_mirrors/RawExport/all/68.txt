# Core principles of “Palliative Care for the Planet”
1. **Suffering-first**: Start from human and non-human suffering as primary ethical concern (physical, psychological, social, cultural, ecological).  
2. **Holistic &amp; interspecies**: Address physical needs (food, shelter), psychosocial needs (grief support, community rituals), and ecological stewardship (habitat refuge, species triage).  
3. **Early, ongoing, and scaled**: Like palliative medicine, intervene early and across scales — individual, community, policy.  
4. **Dignity &amp; agency**: Center affected people’s voices, especially Indigenous and frontline communities with deep ecological knowledge.  
5. **Justice &amp; refusal of false inevitability**: While acknowledging irreversible losses, challenge narratives that normalize injustice or that preclude resistance.  
6. **Non-abandonment + memorialization**: Prevent abandonment by institutions and design communal memorials, archives, and cultural practices to honor loss.  
7. **Resistant care**: Combine comforting/supportive work with strategies that build civic capacity and nonviolent political leverage to prevent further harms.
