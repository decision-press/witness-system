Causal Modality — Yes, It’s Real
At its core, causal modality is the study of how things can cause things, and what kinds of “could,” “must,” and “might” belong to causal relationships.
It’s basically the overlap of:
    • modality (necessity, possibility, counterfactuals)
    • causation (what produces what, under which conditions)
Think:
    • necessary causes
    • sufficient causes
    • possible causes
    • counterfactual causes
    • modal strength of causal claims
It’s the philosophical plumbing behind statements like:
    • “If X hadn’t happened, Y wouldn’t have.”
    • “X could have caused Y, but didn’t.”
    • “X must cause Y under these conditions.”
    • “X is a possible but not necessary cause of Y.”
Basically:
how different kinds of possibility and necessity operate in causal structures.
